# Company Profile: FreshValue Markets

## Basic Information
- **Industry:** Regional grocery retail
- **Stage:** Mature (family-owned, considering strategic transition)
- **Size:** $850M annual revenue, 32 stores, 4,200 employees
- **Business Model:** Traditional supermarket format with emphasis on fresh produce, local products, and community connections
- **Geographic Markets:** Iowa, Nebraska, South Dakota (predominantly rural and small-city markets, 15,000-75,000 population)
- **Strategic Context:** Facing simultaneous pressure from discount chains, e-commerce, labor shortages, and generational ownership transition

## Business Details

### Operations
- **Store Format:** 32 stores ranging from 35,000-55,000 sq ft
- **Supply Chain:** Centralized distribution center in Des Moines, IA (280,000 sq ft)
- **Fresh Focus:** 40% of revenue from perimeter (produce, meat, dairy, bakery)
- **Local Sourcing:** Partnership with 120+ regional farmers and producers
- **Private Label:** 18% of sales, focused on pantry staples and dairy
- **Delivery:** Limited online ordering with curbside pickup in 12 stores (added 2020), no home delivery
- **Technology:** Legacy POS systems, manual inventory management, minimal automation

### Market Position
- **Customers:** Middle-income families, seniors, rural communities (avg household income $65K)
- **Market Share:** 35-60% share in core small-city markets, declining in larger markets
- **Competitors:**
  - Walmart Supercenters (present in 18 of 32 markets)
  - Dollar General/Family Dollar (expanding aggressively, 200+ stores in region)
  - Hy-Vee (larger regional competitor, 85 stores across same geography)
  - Aldi (entering smaller markets, 12 stores in region as of 2024)
  - Amazon Fresh/Instacart (limited but growing in cities over 50K population)
- **Differentiation:** Community relationships, local product selection, personalized service, store-level autonomy

### Financials
- **Revenue:** $850M (2024), flat growth past 3 years
- **EBITDA Margin:** 3.2% (industry avg 4.5%)
- **Debt:** $45M term loan (distribution center expansion 2019), manageable but constraining
- **Capital Needs:** $60M deferred maintenance backlog (refrigeration, roofing, parking lots)
- **Cash Position:** $12M working capital, seasonal fluctuations
- **Profitability Pressure:** Gross margins compressed from 28% (2020) to 25% (2024) due to wage increases and competitive pricing

### Capabilities
- **Real Estate:** Own 22 of 32 store locations (valuable but aging)
- **Workforce:** Long-tenure employees (avg 8 years), strong institutional knowledge, limited technology skills
- **Relationships:** Deep community ties, vendor partnerships, local government connections
- **Distribution:** Efficient DC for current footprint, underutilized capacity
- **Technology:** Significantly behind industry (no AI, limited analytics, manual processes)
- **Leadership:** 2nd generation owners (3 siblings, ages 58-64), experienced operators but limited digital/strategic planning

### Constraints & Risks
- **Regulatory:** Food safety compliance (FDA FSMA), labor law complexity, SNAP/WIC administration
- **Operational:**
  - Labor shortage (200+ unfilled positions, 45% annual turnover)
  - Wage pressure (minimum wage increased 35% in 3 years)
  - Energy costs (refrigeration represents 40% of utility spend)
  - Shrink (food waste and theft at 2.8% of sales, above industry avg)
- **Financial:** Limited capital for technology investment or store remodels
- **Competitive:** Price gap to Walmart/Aldi widening, dollar stores fragmenting basket sizes
- **Generational:** Ownership transition unclear (next gen pursuing other careers), no succession plan
- **Technology Debt:** Systems integration required for any modernization ($15-25M estimated investment)
